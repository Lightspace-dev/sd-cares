About San Diego Cares

In 1979, Rolf Benirschke, San Diego Cares co-chairman, was the placekicker for the San Diego Chargers. After collapsing on a team flight due to ulcerative colitis, Rolf underwent two emergency abdominal surgeries and required almost 80 units of blood. When a local radio station rallied San Diegans to donate blood to replace what Rolf had needed, the community responded in force and the Chargers Blood Drive was born.

During its 38-year history in San Diego, the Chargers Blood Drive collected more than 74,000 pints of blood, impacting up to 222,000 lives in our community.

With the support of local sports teams and athletes, as well as community leaders and businesses, San Diego Cares is set to be an upbeat, uplifting, and, most importantly, life-saving event in our community.

Join the San Diego Padres, San Diego Gulls, San Diego Sockers and Holiday Bowl in showing how much San Diegans care about one another. Give the gift of blood and celebrate the spirit of our community this holiday season.

About the San Diego Blood Bank

The San Diego Blood Bank is a certified 501 c3 non-profit organization serving hospitals throughout Southern California. We are also the primary provider of blood to Rady Children’s Hospital – San Diego. We are the safety net for our regional blood needs. Every year, more than 120,000 units of blood provided by the San Diego Blood Bank are transfused into patients of all ages, socio-economic and ethnic backgrounds throughout San Diego County. These patients are struggling with life threatening medical conditions including cancer and other blood disorders, severe burns, organ transplants, emergency surgery, trauma, childbirth complications and more. The San Diego Blood Bank is also the California Emergency Medical Services Authority (EMSA) designated Southern California Regional Operations Center for the distribution of blood to Southern California in the event of a major disaster or act of terrorism in the State of California.
